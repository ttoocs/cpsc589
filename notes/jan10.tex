\section [Parametric curves]
3D parametric cruve:
Parameter $u$, some function $Q$, which outputs 3 dependent variables (Can be viewed as functions themselves of $u$, indepdnent of eachother)

Ex: $Q(u) = \begin{bmatrix}[\cos(u) \\ \sin(u) \\ u]\end{bmatrix}$\\

2D parmametric ellipse:
$Q(u) = \begin{bmatrix}[X(u) \\ Y(u)] \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}[a \cos(u) \\ b \sin(u)] \end{bmatrix}$
\begin{verbatim}
input: a,b,inc
for u=0 to 2*pi step inc
x=a*cos(u)
y=a*sin(u)
//save,oklit,whatnot here
\end{verbatim}

Note: you can easily very the samples and level of approximation.
Note: Taking the derivative of a parametric representing, you can find the tangent, (or an approximation of it with small step size) easily.
Note: Speed/rate of movement matters:
Ex: $Q(u) = \begin{bmatrix}[X(u) \\ Y(u)] \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}[a \cos(u) \\ b   \sin(u)] \end{bmatrix} $ vs $R' = \begin{bmatrix}[X(u) \\ Y(u)] \end{bmatrix} = \begin{bmatrix}[a \cos(2u) \\ b  \sin(2u)] \end{bmatrix}$ \\
Note: Any papametric representation is not unique, and the tangents vectors are not always the same magnatude.

\begin{definition}[G - continous]
Geometricly continous: $G^n$

\end{definition}

\begin{definition}[C - continous]
C- continous: $C^n$ (depends on the tangent)
\end{definition}

\begin{example}[continous]
$Q(t) = \begin{bmatrix} [cos(t) \\ sin(t) ] \end{bmatrix}$ - Endlessly G-continous.
$G^0$ - A sharp corner. 

\end{example}

\begin{remark}
The issue of parametric representations is in trying to find the curve of a given object: ex 's'.
Another issue is that it uses functions that are computationally complex (sin/cos)
\end{remark}
