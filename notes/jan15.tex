Cubic parametric curve: $\forall u: B_{0,3} + B_{1,3} + B_{2,3} + B_{3,3} = 1$
Is where you cube $(1-u + u)$
and the curve is $Q(u) = B_{0,3} P_0 + B_{1,3} P_1 + B_{2,3} P_2 + B_{3,3} P_3$

These are bezier curves. (Bernstien basis functions)

$$Q_d(u) = \sum^d_{i=0} B_{i,d}(u) P_i$$
$$B_{i,d}(u) = \begin{bmatrix} d \\ i \end{bmatrix} u^i (1-u)^{d-i}$$

Note: The tangent at $P_0 is 2(P_1-P_0)$ and at $P_2$ is $2(P_2 - P_1)$

Note: It has $C^{\inf}$ continuity.
